The $10,000 risk and the paperwork gap

Emergency disconnect scenario

An electrical fault requiring PGE to disconnect the shared transformer costs $10,000 per occurrence — $526/unit across Sutter's 19 homes. This is entirely unbudgeted. Without formal paperwork, Sutter may need Amelia's cooperation to even request the disconnect, adding delay to a potential fire-risk situation.

Prevention: infrared panel scanning

The Amelia board (NFPA 70E certified) scans their electrical panel with infrared every 10 years to detect hot spots before failure. Cost: $500–$1,500. This is the most cost-effective way to prevent the $10,000 emergency. There is no evidence Sutter does this.
